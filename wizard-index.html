About TS-PARTS Roof Rack Brackets

TS-PARTS roof rack brackets are precision-made steel clamps designed to mount roof rack crossbars directly onto the factory roof gutters (drip rails) of commercial vans, campers and 4x4 vehicles. They require no drilling and leave no permanent marks on the vehicle bodywork.

Each bracket is manufactured from high-grade steel, hot-dip galvanised and powder-coated black for long-term corrosion resistance. HD (Heavy Duty) variants use a thicker profile for increased load capacity — recommended for heavy loads or frequent off-road use.

Brackets are available in multiple height variants — 123 mm, 169 mm, 220 mm, 240 mm, 260 mm, 357 mm, and 510 mm — to match the roof profile and gutter depth of different vehicles. The correct height places the crossbar at a consistent level above the roofline.

Brackets are sold individually. A complete rack installation requires a minimum of 2 pairs (4 brackets). For longer vehicles or heavier loads, additional intermediate pairs are recommended. Use the Wizard to calculate the correct quantity for your specific roof length.

Optional accessory handles (v1 low / v2 high) can be added to each bracket to provide a grab handle for loading cargo onto the roof rack. Recommended for high-roof vehicles where reaching the rack requires extra assistance.

Installation Guide

1
Identify your roof gutter profile

All TS-PARTS brackets clamp onto the factory roof gutter (drip rail) running along the top edge of the vehicle body. Clean the gutter surface and check for damage or rust before fitting.

2
Select the correct bracket height

Use the Wizard to match bracket height to your vehicle. Standard-roof vehicles typically use 169 mm or 260 mm brackets; high-roof or pop-top vehicles use 357 mm brackets. Bracket height determines how far the crossbar sits above the roofline.

3
Mark bracket positions along the gutter

Place brackets at even intervals along both sides of the gutter. Recommended spacing: 1 pair per 75 cm of usable roof length. Always position one pair at the front and one at the rear; add intermediate pairs for longer roofs.

4
Fit the crossbar tube

Slide the crossbar tube through the bracket heads across the full roof width before tightening. Ensure the tube overhangs evenly on both sides. Adjust the position of all pairs before any final tightening.

5
Clamp and torque

Slide each bracket clamp over the gutter rail and tighten the clamping bolt. Recommended torque: 12–15 Nm. Do not overtighten — excessive force can deform the gutter rail. Recheck all fasteners after the first 100 km of use.

6
Attach accessory handles (optional)

Accessory handles v1 (low) and v2 (high) clip onto the bracket uprights to provide grab handles for easier loading. Recommended especially for high-roof vehicles. Fit one handle per bracket on the outside face.
